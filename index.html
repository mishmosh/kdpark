Friends of Karen Donnelly Park


Karen Donnelly Park is a small but special green space tucked into the heart of Pennsport, stretching from Dickinson to Greenwich between 2nd and 3rd Streets. Since its establishment in 1978, the park has been a pocket of shade, calm, and community in the middle of South Philly.

The park features a distinctive stainless steel playground with a teeter-totter and climbable dome, checkerboard tables, shaded benches, a paved path, and a wheelchair-accessible entrance.

How You Can Help

The Friends of Karen Donnelly Park are an all-volunteer group of neighbors. We aim to keep the park clean, welcoming, and well-loved for everyone, from young children to teens to adults to seniors. We focus on tending what makes this park distinct: a calm, unhurried green space in the middle of a busy neighborhood.

Here's how you can help:

  • Adopt a task. Some neighbors take on small recurring tasks — picking up trash on their daily walk-through or watering new plants as they get established.
  • Come to a volunteer day. We organize seasonal cleanups and planting days to pick up litter, sweep paths, and freshen up the park. All supplies provided, and all ages are welcome. We usually announce these via posters at the park and a notice to the mailing list.
  • Share your ideas. Have a thought about how to improve the park? Want to lead a project? We want to hear it.

History

Karen Donnelly Park opened in 1978 as a quiet resting place for Pennsport residents, in memory of a former resident of Dickinson Street. It fell into disrepair over the years, and in the mid-2000s the City planned to sell the lot. Neighbors pushed back, and the park was completely rebuilt and rededicated in 2010.
